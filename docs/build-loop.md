# BuildLoop 安闭环

Public page: https://qc-robotics-2026.pages.dev/build-loop/

BuildLoop is a Chinese pitch-deck concept for the Qualcomm robotics competition. It frames construction safety as an exception-closure evidence problem, not a generic AI camera, EHS checklist, or smart-site dashboard problem.

## One-Line Pitch

> 工地不缺摄像头和巡检，缺的是每个隐患的证据闭环。

English positioning:

> BuildLoop turns construction safety signals into assigned, verified, audit-ready closures.

BuildLoop connects Procore/Autodesk/smart-site systems, AI cameras, mobile inspections, robots, drones, supervisor notes, subcontractor remediation, owner reports, regulator exports, and insurer loss-control files into one safety exception ID.

## Problem

Construction projects already have signals:

- cameras, mobile inspection photos, EHS checklists, AI PPE alerts, smart-site dashboards, supervisor notes, subcontractor messages, owner inspections, insurer/risk-engineering requirements, and regulator templates;
- but after discovery, teams still manually chase who owns the item, whether the evidence is good enough, whether the fix was reviewed, whether the issue recurred, and whether it can be used in audit/insurance/claims contexts.

The missing product object is not an alert. It is an evidence-backed closed exception:

- source link;
- location and zone;
- hazard class and severity;
- trade/subcontractor;
- responsible person;
- due date;
- required evidence;
- reviewer;
- before/after proof;
- timestamps;
- export status;
- recurrence history.

## Why Solve It

Safety closure touches multiple commercial priorities:

- regulator-ready hidden-danger closure;
- owner trust and progress payment discussions;
- subcontractor accountability;
- insurer and broker loss-control documentation;
- OSHA/HSE/ISO-style audit evidence;
- claims defense and dispute records;
- lower inspection/reporting/admin burden;
- better cross-project risk visibility.

BuildLoop should not claim short-term injury reduction. It should prove leading indicators: acknowledgement time, evidence-backed closure time, overdue high-risk open-days, evidence completeness, repeat violations, reopened items, and subcontractor closure reliability.

## Why Now

China:

- The 2024-2026 safety campaign emphasizes major-hazard dynamic clearing, hidden-danger rectification, supervision, and closure management.
- Safety production expenses can cover safety informationization, monitoring, intelligent equipment, hidden-danger remediation, and new safety technology, giving BuildLoop a budget route beyond pure IT.
- Shanghai and other provinces are moving smart-site requirements toward platform connection, device online rates, AI warning, and project/government collaborative closure.
- China positioning should use: `智慧工地监管平台接入`, `重大事故隐患动态清零`, `隐患排查整改闭环`, `项目级闭环处置`, `政企协同闭环处置`, `安责险事故预防服务`, `履职留痕`, `安全生产费用`.

Overseas:

- BLS, OSHA, HSE, and EU statistics keep construction safety highly visible.
- OSHA citations and penalty levels make evidence of good-faith correction valuable.
- Insurance, self-insured contractors, brokers, and owners need leading indicators and loss-control documentation.
- Large projects also face rework, schedule, claims, and data-quality pain, making construction risk evidence useful beyond EHS.

## Insight

Hazard detection is crowded. Evidence-backed closure is still fragmented.

BuildLoop should not compete on detecting PPE better than every vision startup. It should consume safety signals from cameras, drones, robots, Procore, Autodesk Construction Cloud, HammerTech, OpenSpace, DroneDeploy, inspections, photos, and AI systems, then turn them into assigned, verified, audit-ready closures.

## Solution

BuildLoop does not replace Procore, Autodesk, HammerTech, SafetyCulture, OpenSpace, DroneDeploy, EHS suites, smart-site platforms, safety managers, supervisors, insurers, or regulators.

BuildLoop sits between them:

1. Ingest safety observations, CV alerts, inspection failures, mobile photos, robot/drone evidence, and field notes.
2. Normalize duplicates into one exception record.
3. Assign responsible contractor/trade with due date and required proof.
4. Collect correction evidence: photo/video, location, timestamp, reviewer sign-off, source-system link.
5. Verify evidence completeness and flag weak proof.
6. Push closure back to Procore/ACC/HammerTech/EHS/smart-site systems.
7. Generate owner, regulator, insurer, and audit packets.

- temporary electrical and housekeeping/trip hazards;
- hot-work permit and fire-watch evidence;
- confined-space permit token in tabletop/demo form only.

## ROI And Pilot KPIs

Annual value:

inspection/admin labor saved + faster correction + reduced overdue high-risk open-days + lower rework/stop-work/claims risk + insurance loss-control value + owner score/bid-score value.

Pilot targets:

- >95% system uptime on selected zones;
- median acknowledgement time under 10 minutes;
- high-risk exceptions assigned within 24 hours;
- 80%+ closure within SLA;
- 30%-50% reduction in overdue high-risk open-days or repeat violations for pilot scope;
- 95%+ evidence completeness for closed items;
- weekly report accepted by safety manager, project manager, owner/supervisor, or insurer/risk reviewer.

## Moat

The moat is an operator-specific construction exception graph:

- source systems and evidence links;
- zone/floor/trade/subcontractor mappings;
- hazard taxonomy and severity;
- required evidence templates;
- reviewer and reopen history;
- repeat exceptions by location and contractor;
- owner/insurer rejection patterns;
- robot/edge-AI episodes and human verdicts.

## Architecture

Engineering principles:

- privacy-first;
- weak-network tolerant;
- human-reviewed closure;
- integration with existing systems;
- evidence retention;
- no automatic compliance or safety claims.

Reference architecture:

- Edge: cameras, mobile inspections, edge AI box, robot/drone observations, optional depth/thermal sensors.
- Scene graph: workers/tokens, equipment, zones, hazard classes, permit tokens, trades, subcontractors.
- Closure engine: dedupe, severity, assignment, SLA, evidence template, review, reopen, export.
- Evidence store: images, video, GPS/zone, timestamp, model version, reviewer, source links, chain-of-custody.
- Training loop: LeRobotDataset episodes, visual labels, false positive/negative reviews, final verdicts.
